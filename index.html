Outbreak timeline
1993 — Four Corners outbreak & HPS discoveryUSA
Navajo adults in New Mexico died of acute respiratory failure. CDC identified Sin Nombre virus (SNV) carried by deer mice. 48 cases nationwide; 56% fatality rate. HPS named for the first time. El Niño–driven rodent population boom linked as root cause.
27 deaths 48 cases New virus identified
1995 — HPS becomes nationally notifiableUSA
CDC required all states to report HPS through the Nationally Notifiable Disease Surveillance System, formalizing the tracking infrastructure still in use today.
Policy milestone
1997 — Chile outbreak, Andes virus documentedChile
HPS outbreak in southern Chile confirmed Andes virus (ANDV). Early evidence emerged that ANDV — unlike SNV — could spread between people, a rare and alarming property confirmed in later studies.
Person-to-person suspected Andes virus identified
2000s — Endemic transmission, South AmericaArgentina / Chile
Argentina reported ~100 confirmed cases annually 2013–2018 across four endemic regions. Argentina and Chile emerged as the global hotspot for Andes virus HPS, with an 18.6% case fatality rate.
~100 cases/yr in Argentina 18.6% fatality rate
2012 — Yosemite National Park clusterCalifornia, USA
10 visitors to Curry Village tent cabins contracted Sin Nombre virus; 3 died. Deer mice were nesting inside double-wall foam insulation of the cabins. ~22,000 potential contacts worldwide were notified. The cabins were demolished.
3 deaths 10 cases
2018–2019 — Epuyén cluster, largest known P2P eventPatagonia, Argentina
A single introduction of Andes virus into a village of 2,000 people in Chubut Province caused 34 confirmed infections and 11 deaths. Three "super-spreader" patients who attended crowded social events drove the chain. The best-documented case of sustained human-to-human ANDV transmission — the reference event for the 2026 cruise investigation.
11 deaths 34 cases Confirmed P2P spread
2020s — Vaccine phase I trials; still no licensed treatmentGlobal
US Army Medical Research completed Phase I clinical trials of an Andes virus vaccine. No FDA-licensed vaccine or antiviral treatment exists. WHO, CDC, and ECDC confirm that supportive hospital care remains the only standard of care for HPS.
Vaccine in trials
Apr–May 2026 — MV Hondius cruise ship cluster Active Atlantic
Dutch polar expedition vessel departed Ushuaia, Argentina, April 1. A Dutch passenger fell ill April 6 and died April 11 from respiratory distress. His wife died April 26 in Johannesburg; a 3rd death followed. As of May 8: 6+ confirmed, 9 suspected; patients hospitalized in South Africa, Netherlands, Germany, Saint Helena, and Switzerland. CDC dispatching to Tenerife. 17 Americans to be repatriated to Nebraska quarantine unit. WHO global risk: low.
